# 📊 RBS MBA Graduate Statistics Analysis

Statistical analysis of the first graduating MBA class at **Rebecca Business School** 
A data-driven approach to evaluating program outcomes, estimating future performance, and improving recruitment strategy.


Rebecca Business School (RBS) is an online startup disrupting traditional postgraduate education. As a newly hired Recruitment Analyst, you have access to data from RBS's first graduating MBA cohort, including:

- **Application details**  undergraduate degree, grade, and work experience
- **Program performance**  MBA final grade
- **Employability scores**  measured before and after the program
- **Employment outcomes**  placement status and annual salary 2 months post-graduation

The goal is to leverage statistics to evaluate this class, predict the performance of future cohorts, and propose data-driven recruitment changes to improve graduate outcomes.



## 🔬 Analysis Sections

The workbook is structured as a full statistics curriculum applied to real RBS data, organized into five major areas:

### 1. Descriptive Statistics
Understand the shape, center, and spread of the data.
- Categorical & numerical frequency distributions, histograms
- Mean, median, mode — overall and by subgroups (e.g. work experience, undergrad degree)
- Min, max, range, quartiles, IQR, box & whisker plots
- Variance, standard deviation, coefficient of variation
- Skewness analysis

### 2. Probability Distributions
Model the data and calculate likelihoods.
- Normal distribution fitting for MBA grades and salaries
- Z-scores and the empirical rule
- Probability calculations using `NORM.DIST` and `NORM.S.DIST`
- Value and Z-score estimation using `NORM.INV` and `NORM.S.INV`

### 3. Confidence Intervals
Estimate population parameters from sample data.
- Margin of error
- Confidence intervals for means (Z and T distributions)
- Confidence intervals for proportions
- Confidence intervals for dependent and independent samples
- Confidence intervals for differences in proportions

### 4. Hypothesis Testing
Draw statistically supported conclusions.
- P-value interpretation
- Two-tailed and one-tailed hypothesis tests
- Tests for proportions, dependent samples, independent samples
- Testing claims about placement rates, salary thresholds, and employability improvements

### 5. Regression Analysis
Predict outcomes using linear models.
- Scatterplots and correlation analysis
- Simple linear regression (e.g. predicting post-MBA employability)
- R-squared and standard error for model evaluation
- Hypothesis testing for regression significance

## 💡 Key Business Questions Answered

- What proportion of graduates were placed within 2 months?
- What is the average salary, and does prior work experience affect it?
- Did the MBA program meaningfully improve employability scores?
- Do Business undergrads outperform others in the MBA?
- Can we predict a student's post-MBA employability from their pre-MBA score?
- Can we prove with 99% certainty that work experience leads to higher earnings?
- What percentage of future graduates can we expect to earn six figures?

All Done in Microsoft Excel
